Research

Currently, I am doing research on Cloud Security and my thesis entitled with “ANALYSIS OF ATTACKS ON CLOUD ENVIRONMENT and SIMULATION OF DDOS”. Here are some points of my research that I want to share with you:

Demystifying Cloud Computing and Cloud Security:
Providing a clear and concise overview of cloud computing, its benefits, and its potential security challenges. Explaining the importance of securing cloud environments and the implications of successful attacks.

DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service) Attacks:
Explored how DDoS attacks work, different types of DDoS attacks (e.g., volumetric, application layer), and their impact on cloud services and websites.

Web Security on Cloud Computing:
Investigating the vulnerabilities that web applications hosted in the cloud may face. Discussed common web application security flaws like SQL injection, Cross-Site Scripting (XSS), and how these can be mitigated.

Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attacks:
Explaining how MITM attacks can compromise data integrity and confidentiality in cloud environments. Discuss encryption and authentication methods to prevent MITM attacks.

Email Bombing:
Analyzed the impact of email bombing on cloud-based email services and the techniques used to carry out such attacks.

RAT (Remote Access Trojan) and Trojan Attacks:
Investigating the risks posed by RATs and Trojans in cloud systems. Discuss best practices for detecting and mitigating these types of attacks.
